  • The argonaut octopus has evolved the ability to produce a floating shell-like structure to care for its offspring. This shell-like structure is made of a mineral formula that is seceted by the octopus using two of its arms. The shell-like structure can grow to nearly a foot in length and can hold more than 40,000 embryos. The argonaut octopus crawls inside its shell-like purse, traps some air bubbles inside, then uses its buoyancy to bob just beneath the surface of water in warm oceans around the world. The argonaut octopus has evolved this ability independently, and not by reactivating archaic genes from the mollusk era. The argonaut octopus’s shell-like egg case didn’t evolve from the ancestral shell, but it is the argonauts’ own evolutionary innovation for a new purpose. The findings also feed into a debate over whether the argonaut’s egg case should really be called a shell. Michael Vecchione of the National Museum of Natural History wishes people wouldn’t call it a shell because there’s a major difference in how it’s constructed. Other mollusks make their shells with mantle tissue secreted by a gland, Dr. Vecchione said, whereas the arm tips of A. argo secrete the argonaut’s shell material. He hopes these novel findings will finally convince people to stop calling it a shell and start a rebrand. Beyond the shell debate, Dr. Setiamarga and colleagues’ new genome sequence may help scientists understand more about how argonauts evolved to be pelagic, or living in open waters, and not benthic, like other octopuses who prefer the depths.

Murakami On Writing

  • A book review of “Novelist as a Vocation” by Haruki Murakami. The reviewer summarizing the book states that it is a collection of essays that offer insight into Murakami’s profession, and that the author is at pains to stress that these are simply his own thoughts and experiences and that he is not really giving advice at all. The reviewer goes on to say that, despite his success, Murakami remains painfully self-effacing, telling us that he is “ordinary” and unintelligent, and he often seems to be baffled (and even troubled) by what he has achieved. The reviewer also states that the book is notably bereft of personal detail and it is filled, instead, with already well-established moments from his life—a handful of stories he has rehashed endlessly in interviews and essays, perhaps hoping to deflect any meaningful inquiry into his life.

  • In short, researchers are suggesting that the GW190521 black hole merger may have been the product of two black holes finding each other in the vastness of space, rather than the product of two massive stars that formed as a binary system. This is based on the fact that the GW190521 signal did not fit templates especially well and only matched best if the black holes involved weren’t spinning at all. The chirp was unusually short, and there is no sign of a signal prior to the actual merger. Finally, both of the objects involved in the merger were relatively massive: about 50 and 80 times the mass of the Sun. Black holes of this size don’t form in supernovae (those typically start out with less than 15 solar masses), so these are likely the products of prior collisions.

Genes and tongues are not always tied together

  • The text discusses the global database GeLaTo, which links linguistic and genetic data, and the findings of a study on the relationship between languages and genes. The study found that there are a large number of matches between the two, but also widespread and systematic mismatches. The mismatches typically result from populations shifting to the language of a genetically different neighbouring population. The study provides new insight into the role of language in shaping human diversity.

The Mystery of Spin

  • The article discusses a paper by Charles Sebens which claims that electrons have “spin” even though they aren’t classical spinning material objects. The article argues that this phenomenon is already present in the single particle theory, and that the quantum field theory just provides a consistent multi-particle theory.
