Physicists find quantum coherence and quantum entanglement are two sides of the same coin

  • The text explains the findings of a study that showed that quantum coherence and quantum entanglement are “operationally equivalent.” This means that they are equivalent for all practical purposes, though still conceptually distinct. The study also showed that coherence and entanglement can be converted into each other. This discovery has several important implications, one of which is that all of the knowledge that researchers have obtained about entanglement can now be directly applied to coherence. The operational equivalence of coherence and entanglement will likely have a far-reaching impact on areas ranging from quantum information theory to more nascent fields such as quantum biology and nanoscale thermodynamics.

The argonaut octopus crawls inside its shell-like purse, traps some air bubbles inside, then uses its buoyancy to bob just beneath the surface of water in warm oceans around the world. The argonaut octopus has evolved this ability independently, and not by reactivating archaic genes from the mollusk era. The findings also feed into a debate over whether the argonaut’s egg case should really be called a shell.
